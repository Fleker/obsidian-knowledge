## Hurricane Ida
When the hurricane came through a few years ago, 11 people drowned in illegal basement apartments. At the same time, these places offer housing for people who otherwise couldn't get it. There is [slow but growing effort](https://www.thecity.nyc/2024/08/30/basement-apartments-hurricane-safety/) to make them legal and aligned with standards for safety.

Code changes:
- Higher ceilings
- More points of egress
- Full windows
- Should there be some water sensor akin to a smoke alarm?

Legalizing basement dwellings is a [YIMBYism](./YIMBYism.md) concept.

A 2019 study yielded some problematic results:

> The [pilot](https://www.nyc.gov/site/hpd/services-and-information/basement-apartment-conversion-pilot-program.page) aimed to convert 40 basements in one part of Brooklyn, but — stymied by specific rules that required costly renovations — resulted in just [one conversion](https://gothamist.com/news/absurd-rules-torpedo-effort-to-make-nycs-basement-apartments-safe-and-legal). It could cost almost $14 billion to convert 50,000 illegal units, the city [estimated](https://www.nyc.gov/assets/cdbgdr/documents/nyc-hurricane-ida-action-plan-08-26-22.pdf).
