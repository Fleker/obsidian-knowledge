A way to tax traffic with an automated fee on vehicles entering a given zone.

## New York City
In 2024 there will be a $15 daily toll to go south of 60th street in <span class="private-link" title="This page is private" style="color: #94a3b8; cursor: not-allowed; text-decoration: dashed underline;">Manhattan</span>. This is expected to raise $15bn for the <span class="private-link" title="This page is private" style="color: #94a3b8; cursor: not-allowed; text-decoration: dashed underline;">MTA</span>. $3bn [must go towards commuter rail](https://x.com/ndhapple/status/1798728228208570440).

The [vast majority](https://twitter.com/saraklind/status/1692262241419030835) of people do not drive into the south of Manhattan. This would be a tax on [a small number of people](https://x.com/mattyglesias/status/1799191321313177799), mainly the rich. [About 1% of workers drive](https://x.com/queens_parents/status/1799107709545115817). A lot of the city [is entirely car-free](https://x.com/bellachu10/status/1798548803555037545). And transportation [is a major source of carbon emissions](https://x.com/bellachu10/status/1810679074718957977). Roughly half of commuters [take public transit](https://x.com/StatisticUrban/status/1876058690681442566).

With regard to outer-borough residents, [a very small fraction would be affected](https://x.com/sam_d_1995/status/1876827000322629887). One [infographic](https://x.com/sam_d_1995/status/1875907650896236840) breaks it down by each borough. And just [4% of poor outer-borough workers](https://x.com/aaronAcarr/status/1878551364088840600) are traveling to Manhattan by vehicle.

There is a problem with [drivers using fake plates](https://gothamist.com/news/nypd-is-ticketing-more-ghost-cars-but-congestion-pricing-could-drive-a-surge-in-new-toll-dodgers) to avoid tolls and police enforcement. This leads to a loss of [over $100m dollars](https://nyc.streetsblog.org/2024/01/22/audit-dot-cameras-miss-millions-of-speeding-drivers) annually. See <span class="private-link" title="This page is private" style="color: #94a3b8; cursor: not-allowed; text-decoration: dashed underline;">Ghost Plates</span>.

The teachers union [abused environmental laws to try halting it](https://twitter.com/UFT/status/1742948763033841832?t=H-YtJ_QZ6qVsu7BA96otFA&s=19) despite [few teachers being subject to it](https://twitter.com/StreetsblogNYC/status/1742960293267828755?t=wfffPKDgu8syaWamlHjV_Q&s=19).

Congestion negatively affects [ambulance response times](https://x.com/Newyorkist/status/1819498681546297454). It is [the optimal way](https://x.com/RepJerryNadler/status/1821654137513165105?t=wVMKXTjEYFs2SOqdWH1pdQ&s=19) to improve the city and public transit.
A movement in the early 2000s which advocated for expanding bike lanes and congestion pricing. They used "guerilla" methods to do things like enforce biking lanes from parking. They'd be seen as gentrifiers today.

## Toll Evasion
There is [a lot of cases of toll evasion](https://twitter.com/AirlineFlyer/status/1762858799671493011) using fake or obscured plates. Gersh Kuntzman goes around [documenting these cases](https://twitter.com/GershKuntzman/status/1778252420138598677). Some toll evaders [are felons](https://x.com/mattyglesias/status/1805205447718129706) and [some may be violent](https://x.com/marcelemoran/status/1801617236534219259).

> "[t]he food purveyor's charge can be made up by adding one-half of one percent to food prices, which seems eminently manageable," Komanoff wrote to Hell Gate. "And I haven't said anything about the trucker/purveyor's time savings due to fewer vehicles on the road—and curbside as well."

## A Pause on Congestion Pricing?
<span class="private-link" title="This page is private" style="color: #94a3b8; cursor: not-allowed; text-decoration: dashed underline;">Hochul</span> announced an "indefinite pause" weeks before congestion pricing was to go into effect. This has led to a groundswell in opposition. She instead [proposed a business tax](https://x.com/bdhowald/status/1798442543618863206), but that didn't get a vote in the legislature before the session ended.

This is leading the MTA to [cancel several projects](https://x.com/ndhapple/status/1799264136234479885). They now have to [re-evalutate](https://x.com/bd_wetz/status/1799259813110755741) what they can work on. It also [prevents federal matching funds](https://x.com/tysonbrody/status/1798562054246465934) for big projects. This [hurts disability in fact](https://x.com/LittleMammith/status/1798410653847453841) as it'll slow down elevator installation. Also, disability drivers got exemptions.

This will [have significant climate implications](https://x.com/robinsonmeyer/status/1798467209116922025).

#### Lawsuits Filed
Two lawsuits [were filed](https://x.com/richmintz/status/1816638155434389822). The only remaining requirement is for the state DOT to sign an approval. The governor has instructed the DOT not to do it.

One lawsuit is filed by City Club and is seeking to force a DOT signature.

The other is by <span class="private-link" title="This page is private" style="color: #94a3b8; cursor: not-allowed; text-decoration: dashed underline;">Riders Alliance</span> [along with EarthJustice and Transportation Alternatives](https://x.com/TransAlt/status/1816511075812036667) [and the comptroller](https://x.com/2AvSagas/status/1813628303892218134), which claims that this violates the original CP law, a law around reducing carbon emissions, and a constitutional right to clean air. That one is [going to begin](https://x.com/NYCComptroller/status/1832089635641503799) on <span class="private-link" title="This page is private" style="color: #94a3b8; cursor: not-allowed; text-decoration: dashed underline;">2024-09-27</span>.

The judge in that case [was stuck in traffic](https://x.com/ChristRobbins/status/1839667899838968167).

In addition, New Jersey [sued](https://www.nytimes.com/2024/12/31/nyregion/congestion-pricing-new-york-new-jersey.html) to halt congestion pricing but it's unlikely that the judge will order a halt.

### Consequences of cancelling
- [Elevator upgrades are cancelled](https://www.thecity.nyc/2024/06/11/mta-ada-accessibility-congestion-pricing-funding/)
- City loses on [2% expected rider increase](https://x.com/saraklind/status/1805342260890157272)
- This means the Second Ave Subway extension is halted
	- This [hurts Harlem residents](https://www.amny.com/news/congestion-pricing-harlem-second-avenue-subway-reaction/)
- Higher expenses for [R211 contracts and replacements](https://x.com/2AvSagas/status/1805981932238307456)
- Stations are [in need of significant climate upgrades](https://x.com/Gothamist/status/1801693599790194807)
- Because the MTA had plans to buy new trains and related work, [over 100K jobs are at risk](https://www.nytimes.com/2024/06/26/nyregion/congestion-pricing-funding-job-loss.html), particularly those upstate
- A NYTimes study showed [$200K in uncollected tolls hourly](https://www.nytimes.com/interactive/2024/07/01/upshot/congestion-pricing.html)
- The state [already spent $1bn](https://www.nytimes.com/2024/07/16/nyregion/congestion-pricing-cost-hochul.html) on setting up the program
- The Times [published an interactive look](https://www.nytimes.com/interactive/2024/07/11/nyregion/subway-cuts-congestion-pricing.html) at impacted stations and lines
- Emergency services like ambulances [are getting worse response times](https://x.com/bradhoylman/status/1837138243222954105?t=Woazse29IW9Vnx0RGuhAqw&s=19)

## Congestion Pricing Results
- This has led to an increase in [public transit](https://x.com/eaconner/status/1877393986643538354) year-over-year
	- Roughly [1-2%](https://x.com/LucasPeilert/status/1878647409372446846)
- This mirrored the [traffic reduction](https://x.com/arlvabikelanes/status/1876032481629241557) when I-66 put in rush hour dynamic pricing
- Parking is already [a form of congestion price](https://x.com/MattBruenig/status/1876275398495105269)
- People like it
	- [Fast trips to the airport](https://x.com/ramit/status/1877355094708027582)
	- The [Q32 Bus](https://x.com/RidersAlliance/status/1876356602749731139) from Jackson Heights was really efficient
	- It's also benefitting [the residents of Jersey City](https://x.com/eaconner/status/1876043463365705980)
	- Limousine driver [is optimistic](https://x.com/sam_d_1995/status/1875918352172093543)
	- The Midtown diner owner [is now taking the subway](https://x.com/aaronAcarr/status/1878970317768630482)
	- Even more people [are quite happy](https://x.com/bfurnas/status/1878138641274343659)
	- Bus driver says [they've seen unprecedented efficiency](https://x.com/bfurnas/status/1877704714587922821)
- People are [making rational economic tradeoffs](https://x.com/courtneycgross/status/1876288420752097460)
	- Police officer on Long Island [decided to take the LIRR](https://x.com/ChristRobbins/status/1876277167870607815/photo/1)
	- Residents in Lower Manhattan [are happy](https://x.com/ChristRobbins/status/1876277167870607815/photo/2)
	- Electrical contractor Maurice DiMaggio [is happy to see less traffic](https://x.com/danisimons/status/1876266467529994258)
	- Someone [saved half an hour](https://x.com/dahvnyc/status/1876278012263682149) from his drive from Montclair
	- The [congestion-pricing-tracker](https://www.congestion-pricing-tracker.com/) is a good resource showing big improvements

	- There was a 35% decrease in traffic [along Canal Street](https://x.com/2AvSagas/status/1878964149553860757)
	- A decrease of [7.5% vehicles](https://x.com/RidersAlliance/status/1878909484770447513), 43K [fewer drivers](https://x.com/Gothamist/status/1878923570501361664)

	- Broadway ticket prices [very high](https://x.com/TweetBenMax/status/1898708708294525402)
- Business foot traffic [is up](https://x.com/JanetLiff/status/1887283131117355230) in congestion zone
