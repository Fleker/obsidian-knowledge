## How wealthy people cheat taxes
- Ultra-wealthy people don't pay income taxes. They can borrow using their wealth as collateral. When they die their children collect that wealth, [exploiting the cost basis loophole](https://x.com/lhfang/status/1826288670938472495) to avoid taxes.
- One paper examines [unrealized gains and borrowing](https://x.com/ZLiscow/status/1882090991609655384)
* [The proposed billionaire tax would tax borrowed amounts as capital gains, offsetting the tax with deductions from stock sales, aiming to address the "buy-borrow-die" problem.](https://x.com/ArmandDoma/status/2006127516629532926)
* ["The fact that the rich move rather than fight the tax shows how little political power they actually have. It also shows how powerful unions and other special interest groups are."](https://x.com/gregorykennedy/status/2032093383372714085)
* [Matthew Yglesias notes that the public perception of billionaires being constantly famous is an illusion, as many of the world's wealthiest individuals, like Larry Page and various Walton heirs, maintain a very low profile.](https://x.com/MalarkeyControl/status/2065892086130372901)

## How much can we get?
* [Jessica Riedl's video on ReasonTV argues that taxing the wealthy, while beneficial, is insufficient to address large budget deficits, debunking the notion that historical high tax rates prove otherwise.](https://bsky.app/profile/jessicabriedl.bsky.social/post/3lyh2cus5bc2m)
* [Swann Marcus highlights the disparity between massive government spending ($17.8 trillion in 2023) and global billionaire wealth, questioning why societal problems persist despite this financial imbalance.](https://x.com/SwannMarcus89/status/1997210225145782537)
- An example of this in 2011 [led to revenue loss](https://x.com/JPubEcon/status/1931475090102997354) as taxable wealth decreased
* [A proposed wealth tax on billionaires could generate significant revenue, potentially reaching $405 billion in 2026, which could be used to fund programs like direct payments to households, healthcare expansion, and affordable housing initiatives, as highlighted by Claire Valdez's support for such legislation.](https://x.com/StatisticUrban/status/2028612894775218431)

## California Ballot Initiative

* [Chamath Palihapitiya warns that California's proposed asset tax is causing a massive billionaire exodus, potentially leading to a significant budget deficit and burdening the middle class.](https://x.com/chamath/status/2010215459522548184)
* [Scott Alexander of Astral Codex Ten critiques California's proposed 2026 Billionaire Tax Act, highlighting how its taxation of unrealized gains and valuation metrics could threaten the Silicon Valley startup ecosystem.](https://www.instapaper.com/read/1991544328)

## Middle Class

* [The discussion highlights that while the upper middle class in America are often undertaxed, the wealthiest individuals actually pay a higher proportion of their income in taxes compared to many European counterparts, as illustrated by tax distribution data. The images show that in 2021, the top 1% paid 25.3% of federal income taxes, while the bottom 95% paid only 8.6%.](https://x.com/nikicaga/status/2006791641022214373)

## Politics

* [Joe Weisenthal notes skepticism that millionaires would significantly leave the US due to higher taxes, referencing Donald Trump's claim that such taxes would be disruptive and cause wealthy individuals to emigrate, echoing concerns about losing wealthy populations.](https://x.com/TheStalwart/status/1915174941986599306)
* [Research suggests that lottery winnings have a minimal impact on voter attitudes, primarily correlating with existing wealth, with a slight tendency towards more negative views on taxes, real estate, and inheritances. This study, based on lottery winners and voters, indicates that wealth-related factors are stronger drivers of political attitudes than lottery outcomes.](https://x.com/captgouda24/status/1915981434054967415)
- [MA FairShare](https://x.com/MattBruenig/status/1990495467567001841) tax raised lots of revenue, without people leaving, and funded projects
- Top 1% to 5% [pay most in taxes](https://x.com/InlandCaGuy/status/1985490804673298496) disproportionate to their cncome
- A massive billionaire tax hike [would raise just 300B](https://x.com/WomanDefiner/status/1984745141836333470)
- In the US, effective income tax rate [is higher](https://x.com/BarneyFlames/status/1999542264637292629) than Europe
- Taxing the middle class [is the biggest tax pool](https://x.com/LinkofSunshine/status/1960743983589499231)
* [Experts debate the feasibility of a $6 trillion tax cut through corporate loopholes and executive compensation reductions, with proposals like Senator Booker's standard deduction potentially reducing federal tax revenue significantly.](https://x.com/AshleySchapitl/status/2031192747512614966)
* [Dr. Thad examines the growing political momentum behind wealth tax proposals targeting billionaires and argues that these seemingly intuitive solutions for wealth inequality are highly problematic upon closer investigation.](https://www.instapaper.com/read/1985167990)
* [The Economist argues that implementing 'Robin Hood' wealth taxes to fix broken government budgets is wrong in principle and ultimately ineffective.](https://www.instapaper.com/read/1983096364)

## Estate Taxes

* [Economists Moretti and Wilson found that billionaire sensitivity to estate taxes increases with age, and while most states are currently on the left side of the Laffer curve, a 50% estate tax rate could lead to significant migration and evasion, potentially being a negotiating tactic.](https://x.com/arpitrage/status/2032507545894412356)
