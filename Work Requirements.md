- A paper found SNAP work requirements [had no effect on employment](https://x.com/jdcmedlock/status/1802089278882955449) but Medicaid means-testing did reduce employment
- * [A Georgia study suggests that Medicaid work requirements, implemented as part of expansion, did not improve insurance coverage or employment compared to states without expansion, potentially influencing nationwide policy.](https://bsky.app/profile/medpagetoday.com/post/3m2hred6gzr27)
	- * [Georgia's work requirements for social programs have led to significantly higher spending on private consultants than on healthcare for recipients, highlighting concerns about the privatization of the safety net.](https://x.com/j_e_brandt/status/2016555085833392628)
* [Recent years show a significant increase in the average weeks unemployed, taking twice as long to transition out of unemployment compared to before 2008, even when the unemployment rate is low, highlighting the need for SNAP waiver flexibility to support workers finding new jobs.](https://x.com/besttrousers/status/1922448789786292342)
