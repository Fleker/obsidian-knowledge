A form of <span class="private-link" title="This page is private" style="color: #94a3b8; cursor: not-allowed; text-decoration: dashed underline;">Public Transit</span> which includes a <span class="private-link" title="This page is private" style="color: #94a3b8; cursor: not-allowed; text-decoration: dashed underline;">Bus</span> network which makes a few stops at a high frequency which can resemble the efficiency of a <span class="private-link" title="This page is private" style="color: #94a3b8; cursor: not-allowed; text-decoration: dashed underline;">Subway</span> system at a lower cost.

> International bus experts on where to create high-speed bus rapid transit (BRT). Factors include: 
> Total corridor ridership
> Connection to other high-ridership bus lines and subways
> Ability of nearby roads to handle rerouted traffic
> Proximity to low-income populations
