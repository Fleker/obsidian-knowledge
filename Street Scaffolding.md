title: "Street Scaffolding"
---
These "sidewalk sheds" are very common in New York City, Manhattan especially.

So the city is trying to [allow temporary art](https://www.nytimes.com/2024/07/15/nyregion/scaffolding-art-city-canvas.html) to beautify them.

When a piece of a facade fell and killed a woman, the city [passed a law requiring facade inspections](https://www.slowboring.com/p/new-york-city-shouldnt-be-covered). If faulty, the owner must install a shed for protection. It ends up being cheaper to maintain sheds than do the actual repair, and the law doesn't have a repair mandate. Peer cities allow buildings to install less sightly nets or differentiate regulations based on building context.

The city [is reforming the rules](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/03/26/nyregion/nyc-scaffolding-sidewalk-sheds-city-council.html) to make them more aesthetic while also reducing the number of them.

* [NYC is developing a plan to expedite the removal of scaffolding, addressing a persistent issue impacting New Yorkers, according to Zohran Kwame Mamdani.](https://x.com/sam_d_1995/status/1980413517829480903)
