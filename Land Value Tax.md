A concept popularized by Henry George to tax the unappreciated value of land.
## Georgism
Henry George saw land rents as the chief obstacle to broad wealth, not labor versus capital.

He proposed taxing the underlying value of land, which would incentivise landowners to make the best use of that land through better facilities and newer housing complexes over empty lots and detached houses.

> In the great cities, where land is so valuable that it is measured by the foot, you will find the extremes of poverty and of luxury. And this disparity in condition between the two extremes of the social scale may always be measured by the price of land. Land in New York is more valuable than San Francisco; and in New York, the San Franciscan may see squalor and misery that will make him stand aghast. Land is more valuable in London than in New York; and in London, there is squalor and destitution worse than that of New York.
