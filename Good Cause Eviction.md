> “New York’s “good cause” law allows owners to raise rents annually, without potentially creating a “good cause” problem, by no more than the lower of either (a) inflation (as measured by the consumer price index) plus five percentage points or (b) 10%. That cap gets redetermined annually. Right now it’s 8.82%.”
